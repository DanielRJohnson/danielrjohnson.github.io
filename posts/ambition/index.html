Bottom of a Cliff

Daniel Johnson
 2022-06-26  285 words   2 minutes 
Contents
  • Situation
  • Application
  • Solution

Situation

It is midday when you arrive at the bottom of a massive cliff. You have the desire to climb this cliff, but you feel that when you are halfway up this cliff you will be exhausted. You can either start now and risk exhaustion or come back tomorrow. However, you have responsibilities and obligations such that the next time you arrive here will most likely also be midday. Climbing this cliff is your passion, but you don’t know when you will ever be able to do it. It is frustrating. The cliff goes dauntingly high, but reaching the top feels like a great reward. What do you do?

Application

This is a real question that I am asking myself. For me, I feel this with my personal projects. I love the design of programming languages, but creating a language from scratch is monumental. My first programming language, Glimmer, was took about three or four months of consistent work. (1-2 hours per day on average while in college) While I love Glimmer, I think I could do a lot better and I want to explore more interesting topics in the field. This is the cliff. The obligations are that I am working a 8am-5pm full-time in-person internship which is very exhausting compared to school. There’s no way that I can get home after a long day of work and say, “I want to spend more brainpower by taking on a massive new project”.

Solution

These are just some of my thoughts. For solutions, I could take small steps on a large project or do something less committal like contributing to open source. It is tough to have the motivation sometimes, but I’ll come out stronger.
