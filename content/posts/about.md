# Hi there! I'm Daniel 👋

## My Background

### 🏫 🎶 🎳

I am from Shawnee, Kansas, where I lived from when I was born in March of 2000
until I was about 19. I had a average but fun upbringing focusing on school activities and getting
fine grades. I did band in school from 5th grade to community college,
and I did varsity bowling through all of high school.

### 💻 🕹️ 🥱

From an early age I was interested in computers after playing lots of video
games. My brother and I were obsessed with Pokemon and YuGiOh, then as a teenager
I played a TON of Minecraft and Skyrim. My friends and I also started to play
multiplayer games almost every night like League of Legends and Valorant.

Eventually I took two programming classes in high school, and I came to know that
I enjoyed programming and logic even with some lackluster teaching. (sorry Mr. H)
With an enjoyment of programming and math being my strongest subject, I had the
general idea that I'd probably go somewhere in the realm of computer science.

### 🎓 📕 🧑‍🏫

After high school, I continued focusing on activities, doing band while getting
prerequisites out of the way in community college. Then my life took a quick turn.

I moved to Lawrence to start a computer science degree at the University of Kansas
which would last from August 2019 until May of 2022.
I didn't challenge myself much academically before college, but that changed at KU.
Through my first semester I learned that I would have to take school very seriously,
so I did just that. I got great grades and got some fun real-life experience along the way
with internships. My favorite classes were Programming Languages, Compiler Construction,
Machine Learning, and Mobile Security. I learned a lot in my undergrad and made some
amazing friends, connections, and experiences along the way.

### 🎓 👨‍💻 🏢

As I am writing this (6/16/22), I am currently doing another internship with the company
I worked for last summer. However, this time I am in person due to relaxed covid restrictions.
I work my 8-5 in office meaning that I get to make many more friends
and participate in tons of fun activities. I've been living in a different city, learning a ton
about development, in-person professional life, and even some random things like how to efficiently
solve a Rubik's Cube! It's honestly been a blast, and will be a short-lived but very different
and fun part of my life.

In the Fall I am starting my masters degree in computer science at KU, and I am so hyped
to see where I can go next. My current guess on where my career might go is 50% chance master's
then industry, 25% PhD then industry, and 25% PhD then stay in academia.

### 🚀 🔭 🔮

While this is not a complete story, I am excited to see where it goes!
